# Facebook & Pinterest Traffic Strategy — Verto
*Last updated: 2026-06-12*

## What's Actually Driving Facebook Reach in 2026

The algorithm has shifted significantly. Organic reach from Pages posting links is nearly dead.
What's working is specific and repeatable.

---

### The 8 Formats That Still Get Organic Reach

**1. Facebook Reels (15–60 seconds)**
Highest organic reach of any format by a wide margin. A Reel pointing to a calculator or quiz
can reach 10–50x more people than a link post. You don't need production quality — phone video,
text-on-screen, talking head. Script: 3-second hook → problem reveal → "link in bio / first comment."

**2. Comment-Bait Posts**
"Comment your province/state and I'll tell you what you qualify for."
"Comment YES if you've ever been hit with a delayed flight."
FB's algorithm amplifies posts with high comment counts. Each comment is a distribution signal.
These posts link to your calculator after the commenter replies.

**3. Personal Story Format**
First-person "I" posts massively outperform all other copy formats.
"I didn't realize my credit score was costing me $80,000 until I ran this calculator."
"I've tried every diet for 6 years. I finally tried this 4 months ago."
The hook must contain a specific dollar amount, time period, or outcome — generics don't perform.

**4. "Hidden Truth" Reveals**
"What your [bank / pharmacy / insurance company / ISP] isn't telling you."
This frame triggers both curiosity and mild outrage — the two highest-sharing emotions on FB.
Works for: credit scores, GLP-1 pricing, rural internet, travel insurance gaps, vaping costs.

**5. Tag-a-Friend Posts**
"Tag someone who's been on a diet for years and hasn't found what works."
"Tag a parent who has a kid on Roblox."
FB's algorithm treats tags as engagement signals similar to comments. Design posts that
have an obvious tag target built into the copy.

**6. Polls**
"Which do you pay more for: A or B?" / "Have you ever had a flight delayed by 3+ hours?"
Polls generate comments. Comments generate reach. The poll answer links to the relevant calculator.

**7. Carousel Posts**
Swipe-through format. Works like Pinterest on Facebook.
Use for: cost comparison reveals, "before/after" financial scenarios, quiz result breakdowns.
Last slide = "Run the full calculation at [URL]."

**8. Facebook Groups (Not Your Page)**
Posting in niche groups — not your own page — is how you get reach without an existing audience.
Target groups: senior dog owners, menopause support, rural internet complaints, weight loss support,
quit vaping, parenting, expats abroad (travel insurance), small business owners (LLC content).
Post value-first (the calculation result, not the link). Add the link when people ask.

### The Post Structure That Works

Every FB post should follow this structure:

```
LINE 1:  The hook — specific number, surprising fact, or "I" statement
         (this is all most people see before clicking "see more")

LINE 2:  The problem setup — 1-2 sentences

LINE 3:  The reveal — what you found, what it means

LINE 4:  The bridge — "I built a free calculator that shows you your number"

LINE 5:  CTA — "Link in first comment" (FB suppresses link posts; put URL in comment)
```

Example (Credit Score):
```
I didn't know my credit score was costing me $84,000.

I thought the difference between a 620 and a 760 score was just "easier loan approval."
It's not. It's the interest rate you get on every loan, car, mortgage — for life.

I built a calculator that shows the exact dollar amount your current score costs you.
Takes 90 seconds to run.

Link in first comment ↓
```

### Platform-Specific: Pinterest

Pinterest traffic is intent-based, not interest-based. People are searching, not scrolling.
Highest-performing pin categories for this niche in 2026:

**Finance:** "How to save money on X" / "X is costing you more than you think" / "Retirement calculator"
**Health:** "Weight loss before/after" / "Menopause symptoms checklist" / "Biological age quiz"
**Home/Lifestyle:** "Passive income apps" / "Side hustle ideas" / "Subscription audit"
**Travel:** "Travel insurance guide" / "Flight compensation rights" / "Packing checklists"

Pinterest strategy:
- Create a static pin for each calculator — the output card design (shows a cost comparison or score)
- Pin to both your own boards AND group boards in each niche
- Pin description should be a search query: "How much is bad credit costing you calculator"
- Link pins directly to the calculator URL, not the homepage

## Content Types Beyond Calculators

### Tier 1: Highest Traffic + Revenue Per Effort

**Interactive Quizzes (Personality Style)**
Different from calculators. These don't do math — they assess "which type are you."
"Which GLP-1 program fits your lifestyle?" (already built)
"Which type of investor are you?" → MooMoo
"What's your weight loss personality?" → GLP-1 or fitness offers
"Is your internet situation 'normal' or are you being ripped off?" → HomeFi
Format: 6–8 questions, personalized result page, offer embedded in result.
FB shares these at 3–5x the rate of a standard article.

**Eligibility Checkers**
"Do you qualify?" is the highest CTR format in affiliate content.
"Does your flight qualify for EU compensation?" (built)
"Does your family qualify for Roblox compensation?" (planned)
"Do you qualify for a GLP-1 program without insurance?" (build this)
"Could you get a personal loan with your credit score?" (build this)
These convert because the user has already self-selected — they clicked because they thought they qualified.

**Comparison Tools (Interactive)**
User selects their situation → ranked comparison table appears.
"Compare GLP-1 programs for your profile" (partially built in quiz)
"Compare personal loan rates for your credit score"
"Compare travel insurance for your trip"
These replace static comparison articles and rank better with AI search.

**"Best For You" Recommendation Engines**
Answer 4–5 questions → get a ranked list of 3 offers, sorted for your profile.
Higher conversion than generic "best of" lists because the output feels personalized.
Build one per major cluster: financial, health, travel, security.

### Tier 2: High Traffic, Longer Build Cycle

**Problem-Solution Articles (First Person)**
"I Tried X for 90 Days. Here's What Actually Happened."
These work for E-E-A-T and for FB personal story posts.
Already strong in your catalog. Need more in: men's health, credit/loans, pet health.

**Free Downloadable Checklists / Guides (PDF)**
"Flight Delay Claim Checklist" — downloadable PDF, captures email, links back to calculator.
"Menopause Symptom Tracker" — Pinterest pins this style heavily.
"Credit Health Audit Checklist" — downloadable, links to credit calc.
Email capture is optional — even without it, these generate backlinks and social shares.

**Decision Trees ("Should I do X or Y?")**
"Should I get an LLC or stay a sole proprietor?" → LegalNature
"Should I get travel insurance for this trip?" → Faye
"Should I try GLP-1 or a diet program first?" → GLP-1 offers
Branching logic pages. Lower build effort than a full calculator. High FB shareability.

**"Myth vs. Fact" Articles**
"5 Myths About GLP-1 Medications (And What's Actually True)"
"The 4 Biggest Myths About Car Insurance Pricing"
"What Most People Get Wrong About Travel Insurance"
Strong on both Facebook (shareable) and Google (featured snippets). Drives cluster authority.

These target long-tail, high-intent searches.

## The Facebook Funnel Architecture

```
FB Post (personal story hook + specific number)
          ↓
    "Link in first comment"
          ↓
    Verto Calculator or Quiz
    (builds trust, personalizes result)
          ↓
    Result page with matched offer(s)
          ↓
    MaxBounty affiliate link
          ↓
    Lead / Sale (payout)
```

The key insight from your CONTENT-PLAN.md: FB → Verto page → offer, never FB → offer directly.
Your pages do the trust work. This also means every calculator needs a clean result page with
a strong CTA, not just a redirect.
