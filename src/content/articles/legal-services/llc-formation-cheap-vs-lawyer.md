## What the LLC Actually Protects You From

Before comparing formation services, it's worth being clear on what an LLC does and doesn't do — because formation quality only matters if the protection is real.

**What an LLC protects:**
- **Personal asset separation:** If the LLC is sued and loses a judgment, creditors can collect against the LLC's assets but generally cannot pursue your personal bank accounts, home, or car. This protection requires maintaining genuine separation (no commingling of personal and business finances, maintaining the operating agreement, keeping proper records).
- **Contract liability:** When you sign contracts as "Your Business, LLC" rather than personally, the LLC is the party to the contract.

**What an LLC does NOT protect:**
- Personal guarantees: If you personally guarantee a loan or lease, you're personally liable regardless of the LLC structure.
- Your own torts: If you personally injure someone while conducting business, the LLC doesn't shield you from personal liability.
- Tax fraud, professional malpractice (in licensed professions), or fraudulent transfers.

The protection level from a $149 formation and a $2,400 formation is identical for standard small business operations — it depends on the structure of the entity, not the cost of formation.

## The Three Cases Where You Genuinely Need an Attorney

**1. Multi-member LLCs with complex economics.** If your LLC has two or more members with different ownership percentages, preferred distributions, vesting schedules, or different voting rights than ownership percentage, an attorney should draft the operating agreement. LegalNature's templates handle straightforward equal-ownership arrangements well; they don't handle complex capital stacks.

**2. Converting an existing entity.** If you're converting a sole proprietorship, general partnership, or corporation to an LLC, the conversion has tax and legal implications that benefit from professional review.

**3. Foreign partners or international operations.** FIRPTA, transfer pricing, treaty treatment — these require specialized counsel. Not what LegalNature covers.

For the clear majority of small business formations — single-member LLC, straightforward professional practice, simple two-member equal partnership — the online formation service produces legally equivalent documents at a fraction of the cost.
