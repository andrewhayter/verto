Legal work covers an enormous range: drafting a lease for your rental property (standardized process in a defined regulatory context) is categorically different from navigating a contested divorce with business assets (requires analysis, strategy, and advocacy). Online legal document services are appropriate for the first category and inadequate for the second.

The problem is that many people either pay lawyers for everything (costly and unnecessary for routine documents) or attempt to navigate complex situations themselves (risky without legal knowledge). Here's how to think about the line.

## The Framework: Template vs. Judgment Work

**Template work** is legal drafting where the outcome is largely determined by established law, state regulations, and standard contractual frameworks. A lawyer's role is to know the template and fill it in correctly for your jurisdiction. These tasks are appropriate for online legal services:

- LLC formation (Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement)
- Single-member and simple multi-member LLCs
- Basic wills for straightforward estates
- Residential leases (state-specific, regulated)
- NDAs and basic business agreements
- Power of attorney documents
- Healthcare proxies and living wills
- Basic business purchase agreements

**Judgment work** is legal analysis where outcome depends on interpretation, strategy, and the specific facts of your situation. A lawyer's role is not just document creation — it's analysis, advice, and advocacy. These tasks require attorneys:

- Contested legal disputes (litigation of any kind)
- Family law with contested custody or significant assets
- Criminal defense
- Complex business transactions (M&A, complex equity structures)
- Intellectual property disputes
- Anything where you need to understand your rights and leverage before committing to an approach

The shortcut: if the document follows a known structure and the main question is "did I fill in the right fields," online services work. If the main question is "what should I do here," you need a lawyer.

## Documents Well-Suited to Online Services

### LLC Formation
Forming an LLC involves filing Articles of Organization with your state, creating an Operating Agreement (not always legally required, but essential for defining member relationships), designating a registered agent, and obtaining an EIN from the IRS.

This is genuinely template work. Every state has specific requirements; online platforms know them. LegalNature guides you through state-specific Articles of Organization and generates an Operating Agreement appropriate for your state and ownership structure.

**Appropriate for:** Single-member LLCs, simple multi-member LLCs with standard profit sharing, most service businesses and small retail businesses.

**Requires a lawyer:** LLCs with complex equity structures, LLCs holding significant IP or real estate, or when members have a history of disputes.

### Simple Wills
A will is a legal declaration of how you want your assets distributed after death, who should administer the estate, and (if you have minor children) who should serve as guardian. For estates without significant complexity, this follows a documented structure.

**Appropriate for:** Single or married individuals with straightforward asset distribution wishes, no contested family dynamics, no trust requirements, and no unusual bequests.

**Requires a lawyer:** Estates over $1–2M (estate tax planning), blended families with potential disputes, business ownership succession, special needs trusts for dependents.

### Residential Leases
Residential leases are regulated at the state level — landlord-tenant law defines required clauses, prohibited clauses, security deposit handling, and eviction procedures. A properly created state-specific lease covers the regulated requirements.

**Appropriate for:** Landlords renting residential properties in their home state using standard month-to-month or annual lease terms.

**Requires a lawyer:** Commercial leases (different legal framework), multi-state portfolios with complex ownership structures, or jurisdictions with unusually complex landlord-tenant law (San Francisco, NYC, etc.).

### NDAs and Basic Business Contracts
Non-disclosure agreements follow standard templates. Basic service agreements, independent contractor agreements, and simple vendor contracts have well-established structures.

**Appropriate for:** Standard confidentiality relationships, freelancer/contractor engagements, basic vendor agreements.

**Requires a lawyer:** High-stakes contracts with significant negotiation implications, agreements where the other party has legal representation, contracts involving significant IP transfer or acquisition.

### Can I form an LLC online without a lawyer?

For most single-member LLCs and straightforward multi-member LLCs, yes. LLC formation is a standardized state process: Articles of Organization, operating agreement, registered agent, and EIN. ## What to Watch for With Online Legal Services

**State-specific accuracy:** Verify the platform generates documents specific to your state, not generic national templates. State law governs most of these documents, and a generic will or lease may miss required provisions.

**Understand what you're creating:** Read the document before signing. Online platforms guide creation, but you're responsible for the content. If there's a term you don't understand, look it up before signing.

**Know the limits:** If circumstances change (divorce, major assets, business dispute), a document created for simpler circumstances may not adequately protect you. Review important documents when your situation changes significantly.
