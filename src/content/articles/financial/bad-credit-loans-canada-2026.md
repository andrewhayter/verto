## Why Canadian Banks Reject People the Lending System Can Actually Serve

Canada's Big 6 banks use automated scoring systems. If your credit score is below approximately 650, their system generates a rejection — regardless of your income, employment stability, or the reason your score is low.

This creates a genuine access gap. A Canadian who went through a medical emergency, divorce, or period of unemployment three years ago may have a 580 credit score today despite being financially stable now. The banks' algorithmic cutoffs don't distinguish between "currently in financial crisis" and "recovered from past crisis."

The Financial Consumer Agency of Canada estimates that roughly 25–30% of Canadians have credit scores below 660. That's 9–10 million people who are effectively blocked from conventional bank lending for personal loans.

The subprime lending market exists to serve this group. These lenders price for the additional risk with higher interest rates — but they lend when banks won't.

## How Lender Matching Works — And Why Soft Inquiry Matters

Traditional loan applications work like this: you apply to one lender, they run a hard credit inquiry (which affects your score), then accept or reject you. If rejected, you apply to another lender — another hard inquiry, another 5–10 point score impact. By the time you've applied to five lenders, your score may have dropped 25–50 points, making future approvals harder.

Lender matching networks like Credit Resources CA work differently:

1. You complete a single application with your financial profile (income, expenses, employment, loan amount needed)
2. The platform runs a soft inquiry to assess your credit profile — no score impact
3. The system matches your profile to lenders in its network who are likely to approve you
4. You review available offers with rates and terms
5. You choose a specific offer and formally apply to that lender — only then does a hard inquiry occur

This sequence means you can see what's available without damaging your credit in the process of shopping.
