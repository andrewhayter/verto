The average American household carries two to three credit cards. Most of those cards are earning 1% back or less. Meanwhile, the right card on the right category can earn **5%** — a 5x difference on every single purchase.

Here's how to stop leaving money on the table.

## Chase Freedom Flex — Best for Rotating Categories

5% back on quarterly rotating categories (gas, groceries, Amazon, dining), 3% on dining and drugstores year-round, 1% everywhere else. The categories typically align with peak spending periods. It's free.

**Annual fee:** $0  
**Sign-up bonus:** $200 after $500 spend in 3 months  
**Best for:** Optimizers willing to activate quarterly categories

## Citi Double Cash — Best Flat-Rate Card

2% back on everything: 1% when you buy, 1% when you pay. No categories to track, no activation required. The simplest path to above-average rewards.

**Annual fee:** $0  
**Sign-up bonus:** $200 after $1,500 spend in 6 months  
**Best for:** People who want set-it-and-forget-it rewards

## American Express Blue Cash Preferred — Best for Groceries

6% back at U.S. supermarkets (up to $6,000/year), 6% on streaming services, 3% on transit and gas. If your monthly grocery bill is over $200, this card pays for its annual fee with cash back alone.

**Annual fee:** $95 (waived first year)  
**Best for:** Families and high grocery spenders

## Capital One SavorOne — Best for Dining & Entertainment

3% back at restaurants, grocery stores, entertainment, and popular streaming services. No foreign transaction fees. Excellent for the social spender who eats out frequently.

**Annual fee:** $0  
**Best for:** Foodies, travelers, social spenders

## Discover it Cash Back — Best for the First Year

5% on rotating categories, 1% everywhere else, PLUS Discover matches all cash back earned in year one. Effectively 10% on categories and 2% flat in the first 12 months. Hard to beat as an introductory offer.

**Annual fee:** $0  
**Best for:** First-year rewards maximizers

## How to Stack These Cards

The power move: use **Chase Freedom Flex** for 5% categories, **Citi Double Cash** for everything else, and **Amex Blue Cash Preferred** for groceries. Many households earn $600–$1,200/year in cash back with this three-card setup.

Check your pre-approval status before applying — it won't affect your credit score.
