## Why the Same Person Gets Six Different Rates

Personal loans are priced individually, not published at a fixed rate. Each lender's algorithm weighs your credit score, debt-to-income ratio, income stability, employment type, and dozens of other factors to arrive at a rate that reflects their model of your default probability.

## How Loan Aggregators Actually Work

Platforms like Money Pup are not lenders — they're matching services. They receive your application, run a soft inquiry to establish your credit profile, and match it against a network of lenders who've agreed to work with their applicants.

The lenders pay Money Pup a referral fee when they fund a loan. You pay nothing to use the matching platform — the lender covers the acquisition cost.

This creates aligned incentives: Money Pup earns more when you find a good match and close a loan, not when you're shown the worst available rate. The better their lender network, the more successful matches they generate.

The soft inquiry pre-qualification means you can check what's available across the whole network without any credit score impact. When you pick an offer and formally apply to a specific lender, that triggers the hard inquiry — but you're applying to one lender at that point, not six.

## The Rate Factors Within Your Control

If the rates you're seeing are higher than expected, here are the variables with the most leverage:

**Debt-to-income ratio (DTI):** Most personal loan lenders want DTI below 40% (total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income). If your DTI is above 40%, paying down a credit card before applying can shift your offers meaningfully.

**Credit utilization:** Accounts for 30% of your FICO score. Getting credit card utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%) can improve your score 20–50 points over 2–3 billing cycles.

**Loan term:** Shorter terms (24 months vs. 36 months) typically get lower APR offers because they represent less lender exposure. Monthly payment is higher, but total interest is significantly lower.

**Employment documentation:** Self-employment and contract work receive higher rates than W-2 employment because income is less predictable. If you're borderline-document-able as a self-employed person, organizing 2 years of returns and 3 months of bank statements before applying improves your outcome.
