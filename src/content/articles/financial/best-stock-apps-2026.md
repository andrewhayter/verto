Investing in 2026 looks nothing like it did a decade ago. Commission-free trading is table stakes now. What separates the winners from the losers is data quality, execution speed, and how well the platform helps beginners not blow up their portfolios.

We spent 60 days testing 14 apps with real money. Here's what we found.

## 1. Fidelity — Best Overall

Fidelity keeps winning because it refuses to compete on gimmicks. The app is clean, the research tools are institutional-grade, and the customer service is genuinely helpful. Zero commissions, fractional shares, and access to 10,000+ mutual funds.

**Best for:** Long-term investors who want real research, not hype.

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.9/5

## 2. Robinhood — Best for Beginners

Love it or hate it, Robinhood is still the easiest on-ramp for new investors. The 2026 redesign finally fixed the clunky options flow, and the new "Learn" tab is legitimately educational. Extended-hours trading is now free for all users.

**Best for:** First-time investors, 18–30 demographic.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.2/5

## 3. Public — Best for Social Investing

Public lets you follow professional portfolios and see what real investors are actually buying. The "Alpha" AI feature summarizes earnings calls in plain English. A breath of fresh air in a sea of noise.

**Best for:** Community-minded investors who learn by watching.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.4/5

## 4. Webull — Best for Active Traders

Webull's charting suite rivals desktop platforms. The paper trading mode is perfect for testing strategies without risking capital. Level 2 data is included free — a feature most platforms charge $30/month for.

**Best for:** Technical traders who live in the charts.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.3/5

## 5. M1 Finance — Best for Automated Investing

Set your target portfolio allocation once. M1 rebalances automatically every time you deposit. It's the closest thing to a robo-advisor with the flexibility of a brokerage. Particularly powerful for dividend reinvestment strategies.

**Best for:** Set-it-and-forget-it investors.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.5/5

## 6. eToro — Best for Copy Trading

eToro lets you mirror the exact trades of top-performing investors in real time. Transparent track records, adjustable risk, and the ability to stop copying instantly. Nearly 30 million users worldwide.

**Best for:** Investors who want to delegate without fully handing over control.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.1/5

## 7. SoFi Invest — Best for the Full Financial Picture

SoFi bundles investing, banking, loans, and credit monitoring into one app. If financial fragmentation frustrates you, SoFi is the antidote. Automated and active options, plus IPO access for SoFi members.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants their entire financial life in one place.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — 4.0/5

---

## The Bottom Line

For pure investing power, **Fidelity** wins. For beginners, **Robinhood** still has the lowest barrier to entry. For automation enthusiasts, **M1 Finance** is unmatched.

Whatever you choose, the best app is the one you'll actually use consistently. Time in the market always beats timing the market.
