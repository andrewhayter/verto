## App #1: Nielsen Pulse — $247 earned / 0 active hours

Nielsen Pulse is the anchor of any passive income stack. Install it, grant permissions for app monitoring, leave it running. Once per year it pays you by check or PayPal.

My 12-month payment: $247 (check mailed on the anniversary of installation). This was on a single phone. If I'd added the iPad — registered as a second household device — the annual figure would have been higher.

**What it does:** Collects anonymized data on app usage patterns — which apps you open, session duration, when you use your phone. Nothing from message content, photos, location, or passwords.

**Privacy concern:** The data collection is exactly what ad platforms already know about you. Nielsen is selling that same information with your consent and compensating you. The trade is transparent.

**Bottom line:** If you have a phone and don't already have Nielsen Pulse on it, you're leaving $50–$250/year on the table for doing nothing.

## App #2: Honeygain — $92 earned / 0 active hours

Honeygain shares unused internet bandwidth — companies use residential IP addresses for market research, ad verification, and web access. Your device routes their traffic through your connection in the background.

My 12-month earnings: $92 — slower accumulation than Nielsen, requiring reaching payment thresholds ($20 minimum via PayPal).

**The bandwidth concern:** Honeygain uses real data from your plan. In 12 months on a home WiFi-primarily-used phone: no measurable impact on internet bill. Worth noting: don't run this on mobile data.

**Earning rate:** $0.10–$0.30/GB shared depending on your location and market demand. Home users in the US/EU earn at higher rates than other regions.
