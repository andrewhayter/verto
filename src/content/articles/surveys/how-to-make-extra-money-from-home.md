## The Starting Point: Passive vs. Active

Everything in this category falls into one of two buckets:

**Passive:** You install something, configure something, or link an account once. After setup, earning happens automatically with no ongoing effort. Lower per-hour returns, but zero marginal time investment.

**Active:** You participate — take surveys, complete tasks, join focus groups. Higher per-hour returns, requires time and attention.

The optimal approach stacks passive (free money left on the table) with selective active (picking the highest-paying tasks, not every survey that exists).

## Tier 1: Passive Income (No Ongoing Effort Required)

### Nielsen Pulse — $25–$50/Month
**Setup time:** 10 minutes  
**Ongoing effort:** Zero

Nielsen Pulse runs as a background app on your smartphone, collecting anonymous browsing domain and app usage patterns — the same type of aggregate consumer behavior data Nielsen has sold to brands since 1923. It does not collect passwords, financial information, or message content.

Payment: $25–$50/month in digital gift cards (Amazon, Visa prepaid, etc.), credited monthly. This is genuine passive income — it runs whether or not you think about it.

Why it's legitimate when most "passive income apps" aren't: Nielsen is a publicly traded company ($2.4B market cap) with audited research practices. Their panel methodology is industry-standard. The data they collect is disclosed clearly. They've been doing this for over 100 years.

**Install Nielsen Pulse** → $25–$50/month, nothing further required.

### Cashback Portals — $20–$80/Month Depending on Spending
If you shop online at major retailers, routing purchases through Rakuten (formerly Ebates), Honey, or TopCashback earns 2–15% cash back on purchases you'd make anyway. Rakuten pays quarterly cash back via PayPal or check. This requires no additional money — only a behavior change in how you navigate to retail sites.

## Tier 2: Low-Effort Active (30–60 Min/Day)

### Prolific — $11.40/Hr Average
Prolific is an academic research survey platform used by universities globally. Unlike traditional survey sites, studies pay set amounts rather than uncertain point values, and Prolific enforces a minimum pay standard (currently £6/hr for UK researchers). Academic studies are generally more engaging than commercial surveys.

**My 90-day earnings:** $203.80 across ~18 hours of participation.

**Sign-up:** Requires honest demographic profile completion. Studies match to your profile — niche demographics (specific professions, medical conditions, life experiences) often yield more study opportunities than generic profiles.

### Survey Junkie — $9.20/Hr Average
The most survey-dense mainstream platform tested. Points convert to PayPal cash at 1:1 (1,000 points = $10), with a $10 payout minimum. Simpler than Prolific, slightly lower effective rate, but higher volume of available studies.

## Tier 3: Occasional High-Pay Opportunities

### User Interviews — $75–$150/Session
User Interviews connects participants with companies conducting UX and product research sessions — 30–60 minute video calls testing software, websites, or concepts. Pay is $75–$150 per session. Sessions are not available daily; availability depends on your demographic profile matching company research needs.

**My 90-day earnings:** $280 from 2 confirmed sessions.

This is the highest hourly rate available in this category. Not a reliable primary income source, but meaningful supplemental income for qualifying participants.
