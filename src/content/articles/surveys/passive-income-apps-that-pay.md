The promise of apps that pay you just for having them installed sounds too good to be true. Sometimes it is. After 90 days testing seven apps, here's what actually paid out.

## The Problem With Most Passive Income Apps

Most apps in this category use the same playbook:

1. Sign up and earn a signup bonus (creates artificial initial excitement)
2. Earn points for daily check-ins, surveys, or behaviors
3. Set payout threshold at $25–$50 (high enough to create friction)
4. Make point-to-cash conversions confusing or unfavorable
5. Reduce point earning rates after initial period
6. Rely on user inertia and sunk-cost fallacy to prevent deletion

After the honeymoon period, effective hourly rates for most point-based apps fall below $1/hour — if they pay out at all.

## Tested: 7 Passive Income Apps Over 90 Days

### 1. Nielsen Pulse ⭐ Best Overall
**Type:** Market research panel — browser and app usage data  
**90-day payout:** $112.50 ($37.50/month average)  
**Setup time:** 10 minutes  
**Ongoing effort:** Zero  

Nielsen Pulse won the test by a wide margin — not because it pays the most per minute, but because it genuinely runs passively. No check-ins, no daily tasks, no streaks to maintain. The app runs in the background, payment arrives monthly in digital gift cards, and that's the entire experience.

What it collects: domain-level browsing patterns and app usage categories (not content, not passwords, not messages). Nielsen is a publicly traded market research company — the same company behind TV ratings. Panel data collection is their core business model, not a side project.

**Verdict:** Install it. The risk-to-reward ratio is the best in the category.

### 2. Rakuten (Cashback)
**Type:** Online shopping cashback portal  
**90-day payout:** $67.40 (depends entirely on shopping volume)  
**Setup time:** 5 minutes  
**Ongoing effort:** Navigate through Rakuten before shopping online  

Not a traditional app you "install and forget" — you navigate to Rakuten, click your retailer, then shop as normal. But for consistent online shoppers at major retailers, the 2–15% cashback on purchases you'd make anyway is genuine passive-adjacent income.

Pays quarterly via PayPal or check. No points manipulation — cash back is cash back.

**Verdict:** Worth installing if you buy things online regularly. Irrelevant if you mostly shop in person.

### 3. Panel App
**Type:** Location-based consumer research  
**90-day payout:** $14.25  
**Setup time:** 5 minutes  
**Ongoing effort:** Minimal (occasional check-in for bonus)  

Panel App runs in the background collecting location data and spending you time at various businesses — the kind of foot traffic data retailers pay for. It pays in points redeemable for gift cards. Legitimate but lower earnings than Nielsen Pulse.

**Concern:** Location tracking is broader than Nielsen's data collection. If you're comfortable with location data being used for retail analytics, it's fine. If location privacy matters, skip it.

**Verdict:** Decent supplemental app alongside Nielsen Pulse.

### 4–7. The Apps That Didn't Deliver

**InboxDollars:** Legitimate but $30 payout minimum creates real friction. Average effective earnings: $4–$6/month passively. Mainly a survey app, not passive.

**Swagbucks:** Requires active participation for meaningful earnings. Passive earning (videos in background) returned $8 over 90 days — not worth the battery drain.

**MobileXpression:** Concept similar to Nielsen, smaller company. Payout: $9 in gift cards over 90 days, less reliable scheduling.

**Current:** Debit card rewards app. Requires behavior change (switching bank/debit card) for modest returns. Not recommended unless you want to switch banks anyway.
