## The Legal Framework: Why Sweepstakes Are Different From Gambling

US federal law defines illegal gambling as an activity with three elements: **consideration** (something of value paid), **chance** (random outcome), and **prize** (award of value). Remove any one element and you have a legal promotional activity.

Sweepstakes remove the **consideration** requirement through the alternative method of entry: anyone can enter without paying by sending a stamped envelope to the sweepstakes address (or, in digital versions, by requesting free coins via the free daily bonus or mail-in form). This is why every commercial sweepstakes — Coca-Cola's promotions, radio call-in contests, and online sweepstakes casinos — carries the phrase "no purchase necessary to enter."

This isn't a loophole or a gray area. It's the established legal framework that has governed US promotional sweepstakes for decades and has been validated in courts repeatedly.

The key distinction for sweepstakes casinos: the games use virtual currency (Sweeps Coins), not real money. You play casino-style games using coins you've accumulated; those coins can then be redeemed for prizes. You can always accumulate coins without purchasing — making the consideration element absent by law.
