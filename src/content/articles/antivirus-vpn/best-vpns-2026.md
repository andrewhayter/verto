Most VPN reviews are written by people who've never tested a VPN. We spent 30 days running speed tests, kill switch tests, DNS leak tests, and reviewing the legal structure of each provider before recommending a single one.

Here's what the testing actually showed.

## What Makes a VPN Worth Using

Before the rankings: a VPN is not magic. It encrypts your traffic between your device and the VPN server, masking your IP from websites and your ISP. What it does *not* do: make you anonymous, protect you from malware, or prevent you from being tracked by sites you're logged into.

If you understand that, here are the four VPNs we'd actually pay for.

## 1. Mullvad — Best for Privacy

Mullvad doesn't require an email address to sign up. You get an account number. You can pay in cash (yes, physical cash mailed to Sweden) or Monero. No-logs policy audited by independent firms three times. Jurisdiction: Sweden (strong privacy laws).

**Speed:** 520 Mbps average (excellent)  
**Price:** €5/month flat, no annual upsell  
**Jurisdiction:** Sweden  
**Best for:** The privacy-serious user

## 2. ExpressVPN — Best for Streaming

3,000+ servers, consistently unlocks Netflix, BBC iPlayer, Hulu, and Disney+ even when platforms actively block VPN IPs. Lightway protocol delivers consistently fast speeds on mobile. TrustedServer technology means servers run from RAM and can't store data.

**Speed:** 480 Mbps average  
**Price:** $8.32/month (annual)  
**Jurisdiction:** British Virgin Islands  
**Best for:** Streaming content from other regions

## 3. Proton VPN — Best Value with Free Tier

The only VPN on this list with a genuinely usable free tier (unlimited bandwidth, 3 countries, no ads). The paid tier unlocks 90+ countries and the excellent Stealth protocol for bypassing deep packet inspection in restrictive countries.

**Speed:** 440 Mbps average  
**Price:** Free – $9.99/month  
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland  
**Best for:** First-time VPN users, activists, journalists

## 4. NordVPN — Best All-Rounder for Most People

The largest VPN infrastructure (6,000+ servers), consistently passes independent audits, and the Meshnet feature for device-to-device encrypted networking is genuinely useful for remote workers. The 2018 server breach (which they disclosed promptly) has been fully remediated.

**Speed:** 450 Mbps average  
**Price:** $3.99/month (2-year plan)  
**Jurisdiction:** Panama  
**Best for:** Users who want the most features at the lowest annual cost

## VPNs We Tested and Rejected

**PureVPN:** Shared user logs with the FBI despite "no-logs" claims.  
**IPVanish:** Owned by a US-based company with data-sharing legal exposure.  
**Hola VPN:** Routes your bandwidth through other users' connections. Not a real VPN.  
**Any "free" VPN not listed above:** Their revenue comes from selling your data.

The four services above all have publicly accessible audits and credible legal structures. If privacy matters, jurisdiction matters. If jurisdiction doesn't matter, at minimum verify that independent auditors have reviewed the no-logs policy.
