## Flights

**1. Book on Tuesdays at 1pm ET**  
Airlines often release sale fares Monday night for competitors to match by Tuesday. The sweet spot for lowest fares is Tuesday afternoon. Not a myth — we've A/B tested this across 200+ bookings.

**2. Use Google Flights' price calendar view**  
The ±3 day flexibility view consistently reveals $80–$200 differences for the same route. For a family of four, shifting departure by two days is worth 2–3 hours of flexibility.

**3. Position yourself in a hub city**  
If you live near a small regional airport, build in a low-cost positioning flight to a major hub. LAX, ORD, JFK, ATL, and DFW have dramatically more competitive pricing for international departures.

**4. Set fare alerts, not just searches**  
Hopper, Google Flights, and Kayak all offer alerts. Set them 8–12 weeks before departure for domestic, 3–6 months for international.

**5. Search error fares on Secret Flying and AirfareWatchdog**  
Error fares (typos in airline pricing systems) can slash fares 50–80%. They close within hours. These sites surface them as they appear.

## Hotels

**6. Book direct after finding the rate on an OTA**  
Find the rate on Booking.com or Expedia, then call the hotel directly. Most will match or beat the OTA rate to avoid the 15–25% commission. Ask for "best available rate."

**7. Ask for a room upgrade at check-in — quietly**  
Walking up to a busy front desk and quietly mentioning it's a special occasion works far more often than people assume. The optimal time: weekday afternoon when business travelers haven't yet checked in.

**8. Understand peak vs. off-peak seasons by region**  
Bali in July is monsoon-adjacent and half the price. The French Alps in November is ski-prep and deeply discounted. Research the local definition of off-peak — it's rarely what you'd assume.

**9. Hotel credit cards with anniversary night perks**  
The Hyatt credit card's free night certificate (worth $150–$400) more than offsets the annual fee for frequent Hyatt guests. Similar math for Marriott and IHG cards.

## General

**10. Travel with carry-on only — always**  
The average business traveler saves 45 minutes per trip avoiding baggage claim. Budget airline baggage fees add $60–$100 round trip. A well-packed 40L backpack handles 10+ days of travel.

**11. Get a card with no foreign transaction fees before traveling**  
Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture, Schwab Investor Checking — all zero foreign fees. The standard 3% adds up to $90 on a $3,000 trip.

**12. Use ATMs at the destination, not currency exchange booths**  
Airport currency kiosks charge 7–15% margins. Bank ATMs charge your home bank's fee (typically $0–$5). The effective difference on a $1,000 cash withdrawal can be $100+.
