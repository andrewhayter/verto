## Month-by-Month Feedback From Recipients

### Wine Club (my parents, 4 months)

**Month 1:** French Côtes du Rhône (red) + Spanish Verdejo (white). My parents' reaction: "We wouldn't have bought these ourselves but we'd buy them again." That's the curation value.

**Month 2:** Oregon Pinot Noir + Portuguese Vinho Verde. My dad called to tell me about the Pinot. He doesn't normally call about wine.

**Month 3:** Argentine Malbec + German Riesling. Both solid. The Riesling was the most interesting discovery — off-dry style, unusual for their usual palate.

**Month 4 (bonus — I extended the subscription):** California Zinfandel + New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc. My mother's new favorite white wine is the NZ Sauv Blanc. She bought two more bottles directly.

**Overall verdict from parents:** Would accept a renewal if I offered.

### Craft Beer Club (brother-in-law, 3 months)

**Month 1:** 12 cans/bottles, mix of IPAs, stouts, and sours from 6 different micro-craft breweries. His feedback: "Five of the twelve I'd never heard of. Three were excellent." The ratio is good for a discovery product.

**Month 2:** Different regional focus — some East Coast, some Pacific Northwest. His favorite: a hazy IPA from a Vermont brewery he'd never encountered. He looked them up and ordered a case.

**Month 3:** More IPA-forward. Feedback: "A little repetitive on style — I'd have liked more variety." This is the preference gap that advance questionnaires help with; he prefers mixed styles.

### Gourmet Food Club (colleague, 2 months)

**Month 1:** Hot honey, single-origin jam, artisan crackers, smoked sea salt, flavored olive oil. Her feedback: "Really nice, would have bought half of these anyway." Presentation was gift-ready.

**Month 2:** More ordinary. Branded items she recognized from Whole Foods. Less discovery value. One "meh" month in two.
