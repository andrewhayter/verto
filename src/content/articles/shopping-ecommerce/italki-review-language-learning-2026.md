## Two Years of Duolingo: What It Taught Me and What It Didn't

I completed the Spanish Duolingo course twice in two years. I had a solid 1,200-word vocabulary, understood the basic tense structures, could identify meaning from text. What I couldn't do: hold a 5-minute conversation.

The Duolingo problem is the production problem. The app teaches you to recognize patterns and select correct answers. It doesn't put you in a position where you must *produce* language under the pressure of a real listener who needs to understand you.

There's a documented phenomenon in SLA (Second Language Acquisition) research called the comprehension-production gap: people can understand far more of a language than they can produce. Duolingo widens this gap by optimizing for recognition (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank) over production (open-ended speaking).

I had a 1,200-word Duolingo vocabulary I couldn't access conversationally.

## What to Look For When Choosing a Tutor

**For beginners:** Professional teacher with structured curriculum experience. Community tutors assume more baseline competence.

**For intermediate learners:** Community tutors for conversation practice. Look for: similar time zone (scheduling matters), comfortable with explicit error correction (not all tutors are), experience with your native language background.

**Trial lessons:** iTalki allows short trial lessons with new tutors. Book 3–4 trials before committing to a regular tutor. The tutor-student fit matters more than credentials.

**Red flags:** Tutors who only confirm what you say correctly without correcting errors (positive feedback feels good but slows acquisition); tutors who switch to English when you struggle (removes the production pressure); tutors who have no structure and just chat (fine for advanced learners, insufficient for beginners).
