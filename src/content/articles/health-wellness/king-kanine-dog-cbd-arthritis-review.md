## The Dog, the Diagnosis, and the Starting Point

Guinness is a 9-year-old male Labrador retriever, 28kg. Hip dysplasia diagnosed at age 5 by X-ray — bilateral, moderate grade. He has been on Carprofen intermittently since age 7; we reduced to every-other-day dosing last year to reduce liver stress.

Baseline behavioral observations before CBD:
- Stairs: routinely stopped 2–3 times on the 14-step staircase to our second floor
- Walk duration: tired and sat down after approximately 20 minutes on flat ground
- Morning stiffness: noticeable hesitation and stiff gait for first 10–15 minutes after waking
- Sleep: frequently repositioned during the night, occasionally whimpered during repositioning

I documented these with a simple behavioral log — frequency counts per day for observable events.

## What CBD Does and Does Not Do for Dog Arthritis

**Does:**
- Reduce pain perception through endocannabinoid system modulation (CB1 and CB2 receptor activity)
- Reduce inflammation through secondary anti-inflammatory mechanisms
- Improve mobility outcomes measurably in RCT conditions (Cornell 2019, p<0.001)

**Does not:**
- Reverse joint degeneration — X-ray pathology is unchanged by CBD
- Replace veterinary diagnosis and treatment for severe arthritis
- Produce the same degree of pain relief as prescription NSAIDs for severe cases
