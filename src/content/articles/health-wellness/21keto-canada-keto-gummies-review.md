## What BHB Is and How It Works

Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) is a ketone body produced by the liver during fat metabolism. Under normal dietary conditions, glucose is the body's primary fuel. When carbohydrate intake drops below approximately 50g per day, the liver converts fatty acids into ketone bodies — including BHB, acetoacetate, and acetone — which the brain and muscles can use as fuel.

Exogenous BHB supplements supply BHB from outside the body, bypassing the need for dietary restriction. The BHB is typically supplied as a mineral salt: calcium BHB, sodium BHB, or magnesium BHB, or a combination. Gummy format delivers the BHB alongside carbohydrates from the gummy base, which creates a small but measurable conflict with ketone absorption efficiency.

**The key mechanism:** BHB activates HCAR2 receptors, which have appetite-suppressing and anti-inflammatory effects. A 2020 study in *Obesity* (n=60) found that exogenous BHB supplementation reduced self-reported hunger by 21% over 4 hours compared to placebo. The appetite effect is real. The magnitude is modest.

## The Keto Flu Question

One legitimate use case for exogenous BHB supplements: transitioning to a ketogenic diet. The "keto flu" — fatigue, headaches, and irritability during the first 3–7 days of dietary ketosis onset — occurs partly because the brain is transitioning to a new fuel source while the liver ramps up ketone production. Supplying exogenous BHB during this transition provides an immediate fuel bridge and measurably reduces transition symptoms in several small studies.

If you are starting a ketogenic diet, taking BHB supplements during the first two weeks is supported by the mechanism. As a permanent supplement for someone not following a ketogenic diet, the benefit is smaller.

## What 21KETO Does Not Tell You

Every BHB gummy product faces the same challenge: the gummy base contains carbohydrates. A standard gummy formulation includes approximately 3–5g of carbohydrates per serving from the gelatin, sugar alcohols, or natural sweeteners used. This is not enough to knock a strict keto dieter out of ketosis, but it does create a minor paradox — you are consuming a small amount of carbohydrates while the BHB counteracts them.

This is not a 21KETO-specific failure — it is an inherent limitation of the gummy delivery format for BHB. Powder or capsule forms deliver BHB with zero carbohydrate content. For strict ketogenic dieters tracking macros precisely, powder is more consistent.
