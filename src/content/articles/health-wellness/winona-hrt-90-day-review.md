## Why I Waited Three Years

I first noticed perimenopausal symptoms at 46. Hot flashes started mildly — a few per week, easy to dismiss. By 48, they were 7–9 per day, including 3–4 at night. My sleep, which had always been reliable, became fragmented. I'd wake at 2am drenched, fall back asleep at 4am, and feel exhausted by 11.

My GP offered two things: "it's normal" and a referral to a gynaecologist with a 6-month wait. The internet offered conflicting information about HRT risks, most of it based on the 2002 Women's Health Initiative study, which has since been substantially reanalysed and partially retracted in its original conclusions.

I waited three years because the information landscape was confusing. I went to Winona after a friend who'd been on their protocol for a year showed me the 2022 Menopause Society guidelines update — which unambiguously states that HRT benefits outweigh risks for most women under 60 — and told me to stop waiting.

## What Didn't Change

Weight: unchanged. HRT does not cause weight loss or gain in most women. Perimenopausal weight changes are driven by metabolic shifts that HRT partially moderates but doesn't reverse.

Libido: improved slightly but not dramatically. This is the most variable symptom — testosterone supplementation (not part of standard Winona protocol) more directly addresses libido. Worth a conversation with your Winona physician if this is a primary concern.

Skin changes: minor improvement in elasticity over 90 days, but I wasn't tracking this carefully.
