## The Core Reality in 2026

**Facebook:**
- Page organic reach: 1–6% of followers (nearly worthless without ad spend)
- Group organic reach: 20–40% of members per post
- A post in a 10,000-member active group reaches 2,000–4,000 people organically
- Reels views on Facebook doubled in H2 2025 — it's the #1 reach multiplier right now
- The Andromeda algorithm now ranks: dwell time > saves > shares > comments > likes
- Reposts, stitches, and unoriginal content are actively demoted
- Link posts are suppressed in feed — links belong in the first comment

**Pinterest:**
- 600M+ monthly active users as of 2025
- 85% of weekly users make purchases based on pins they discovered
- Video views up 240% YoY — highest growth format
- Traffic compounds: expect 60–90 days to first meaningful clicks, 9–18 months to full momentum
- Functions as a search engine, not a social feed — pins rank for months, not hours
- One piece of content → 3–5 pin variations → traffic for 12–18 months

**The opportunity:** Only 35% of content marketers actively repurpose across platforms.
One Verto calculator or article can become 10–15 separate social assets with the right system.

## The Syndication System: One Asset → Many Channels

Every piece of Verto content maps to a specific asset tree. Build this once per content piece.

```
VERTO ASSET (calculator / article / quiz)
│
├── FACEBOOK
│   ├── Reel (15–45 sec, screen capture or talking head, links to calc)
│   ├── Group post — personal story hook (value-first, link in comment)
│   ├── Comment-bait post (designed for comment volume → algorithmic boost)
│   └── Carousel post (3–7 slides: problem → data → reveal → CTA)
│
└── PINTEREST
    ├── Static pin 1 — "result card" format (shows cost comparison output)
    ├── Static pin 2 — "headline stat" format (bold number, dark bg)
    ├── Static pin 3 — checklist/step format (for article content)
    ├── Video pin — 6–15 sec animated result reveal
    └── Idea Pin — 3–5 frame walkthrough of the calculator flow
```

Estimated output per Verto asset: **8–12 distributable social pieces**
Time to create with AI tools: **45–90 min per asset**

### The 5 Facebook Post Formats That Drive Reach in 2026

**Format 1: Personal Revelation (highest share rate)**
```
[Specific dollar amount or outcome — this is all they see before "see more"]

[2-sentence setup: what you thought was true]

[The reveal: what you found when you actually calculated it]

[Bridge: "I built a free tool that shows you your number in 90 seconds"]

[CTA: "Link in first comment ↓"]
```

Example (Credit Card Interest Drain):
> I was paying $4,800/year in credit card interest. On purchases I already made years ago.
>
> I knew I had balances. I didn't know what they were actually costing me monthly.
>
> I ran a calculator that added up every card, every APR, and showed me the real number.
> The consolidation loan option would save me $3,100 this year alone.
>
> Calculator link in first comment if you want to run your numbers ↓

---

**Format 2: Comment-Bait (highest comment volume → algorithm boost)**
```
[Question with an obvious personal answer]
[Why the answer matters — one sentence]
[Invitation to comment their specific situation]
[Promise to reply with something useful]
```

Example:
> How many subscription services are you currently paying for?
>
> Most people who add them up are surprised by the annual total.
>
> Comment your number — I'll calculate what that money would be worth if invested for 10 years instead.

---

**Format 3: Hidden Truth (highest outrage/share rate)**
```
[Contrarian claim or "what they're not telling you" hook]
[The proof or mechanism — 2-3 sentences]
[What to do about it]
[Link to calculator/article]
```

Example:
> Your pharmacy is not required to tell you that compounded GLP-1 exists.
>
> Brand Ozempic: $1,186/month. Compounded Tirzepatide (same mechanism): $179/month.
> The telehealth programs that prescribe it are fully legal and run by licensed physicians.
>
> Here's a full cost comparison: [link in first comment]

---

**Format 4: Tag-a-Friend (viral coefficient multiplier)**
```
[Describe a person who would find this useful]
[The value they'd get]
[Tag prompt]
```

Example:
> Tag someone who has been on a diet for 3+ years without finding something that sticks.
> This GLP-1 cost calculator might change the math for them — it starts at $179/month.

---

**Format 5: Facebook Reel Script (15–45 seconds)**
```
0:00–0:03  HOOK — say the number or outcome out loud, on screen
0:03–0:10  PROBLEM — one sentence about what most people don't know
0:10–0:25  REVEAL — show the calculator or result on screen (screen record)
0:25–0:40  CTA — "link in bio" or "link in first comment" with text overlay
```

Reel production: no studio needed. iPhone, natural light, 720p minimum.
Show the calculator running — screen capture is the most authentic format.
Captions ON (85% of Facebook video is watched without sound).

## Pinterest: The Long-Game System

### Why Pinterest Is Different

Pinterest is not a social platform — it's a visual search engine.
- Pins rank in search for 6–18 months (not hours like FB posts)
- Traffic compounds: the more you post, the more your older pins get found
- Users arrive with purchase intent — they're searching for solutions, not being interrupted
- 85% of weekly users have made a purchase based on a pin

Pinterest traffic requires patience (60–90 day lag) but generates passive, evergreen income.
The play: build a large pin library in 2026 that drives consistent traffic in 2027 and beyond.

### The 5 Pin Formats for Verto Content

**Format 1: Result Card (static pin — best for calculators)**
Design: Shows a sample calculator output. Dark background, large numbers, red accent.
Example: "Your 620 credit score is costing you $84,322 over a mortgage lifetime"
CTA overlay: "Calculate your number →"
Link: directly to the calculator URL
Conversion: highest click-through rate of any pin format for this niche

**Format 2: Headline Stat (static pin — best for article traffic)**
Design: One massive statistic on a bold background. Minimal text.
Example: "85% of people on a GLP-1 waitlist don't know there's a $179/month alternative"
CTA: small "Read more →" at bottom
Link: to the article

**Format 3: Checklist/Step Format (static pin — best for guide/article content)**
Design: Numbered list, clean layout, easy to read small
Example: "5 Signs You're In Perimenopause (That Doctors Often Miss)"
Link: to the menopause article or HRT guide
Boards: Women's Health After 40

**Format 4: Video Pin (6–15 seconds — highest engagement)**
Content: animated version of a calculator result being revealed
Or: screen record of the calculator running with a result populating
Format: vertical (9:16), loop-friendly (no hard ending — loops seamlessly)
Captions: required — most Pinterest video plays without sound
Link: to the calculator

**Format 5: Idea Pin (3–5 frames — builds followers, no outbound link)**
Use for: educational walkthroughs, "how this works" content
Frame 1: Hook stat or question
Frame 2–4: The explanation or steps
Frame 5: "Get the full calculator at verto.com/calculators/[slug]"
Note: Idea Pins don't link out but drive profile follows and brand recognition.
Use for clusters where you want to build audience, not just clicks.

### Pinterest SEO — The Keyword System

Every pin needs keywords in 3 places: **title**, **description**, **board name**.

**Title format:**
`[Primary Keyword] | [Secondary Benefit] | [Year or Context]`
Examples:
- "Credit Score Cost Calculator | See How Much Bad Credit Costs You | 2026"
- "GLP-1 Weight Loss Cost | Ozempic vs Compounded Tirzepatide | Free Tool"
- "Menopause HRT Cost Calculator | What Symptom Management Actually Costs"

**Description format (300–450 characters):**
```
[2 sentences of value — what the pin shows/does]
[1 sentence with secondary keywords]
[CTA with URL or "link in profile"]
```
Example:
> Calculate exactly how much your current credit score is costing you in loan interest,
> mortgage rates, and car financing over your lifetime. Compare 620 vs 760 scores side by side.
> Free credit score cost calculator — no email required. See your number in 90 seconds.

**Keyword research tools (free):**
- Pinterest search bar autocomplete (type your topic, note the suggestions)
- Pinterest Trends (trends.pinterest.com) — shows what's being searched right now
- Pin titles of top-performing competitor pins in your niche

### Pinterest Posting Schedule

**Volume target:** 5–10 pins/day for the first 90 days (Pinterest rewards active accounts)
This sounds like a lot — but 3–5 pin variations per Verto asset makes this achievable.

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| Morning (7–9am) | Schedule 3 new pins via Tailwind |
| Afternoon (12–2pm) | Schedule 2 repins (third-party relevant content) |
| Evening (6–8pm) | Schedule 2 new pins from different boards |

**Tailwind** is the official Pinterest scheduling partner. Use it to:
- Schedule pins in bulk across multiple boards
- Auto-post at optimal times for your audience timezone
- Track which pins are driving actual traffic (UTM parameters)

**UTM parameters on every pin URL:**
`/calculators/credit-score-cost?utm_source=pinterest&utm_medium=pin&utm_campaign=credit-static-1`
This tells you exactly which pin format and board is converting.

## The Cross-Platform Syndication Workflow

For each new Verto asset published, run this workflow:

### Day 0 (Publish)
- [ ] Verto calculator or article goes live
- [ ] Add UTM parameter variants to the URL (fb-group, fb-reel, pinterest-static, etc.)

### Day 1 (Facebook)
- [ ] Write personal revelation post → post in 2–3 niche groups (no link — add in comment)
- [ ] Record 30-second Reel showing the calculator result → post to Page + profile
- [ ] Write comment-bait post → schedule for 48 hours later in your own group

### Day 2–3 (Pinterest — batch create)
- [ ] Create 3 static pin designs (result card, headline stat, checklist)
- [ ] Create 1 video pin (15-second screen record or animation)
- [ ] Write keyword-optimized titles and descriptions for all 4
- [ ] Schedule across relevant boards (not all at once — spread over 5–7 days)

### Day 7 (Facebook follow-up)
- [ ] Post the carousel version (3–7 slides) on the Page
- [ ] Write a tag-a-friend post using the result from the calculator
- [ ] Post in a different set of niche groups (rotate, don't spam the same ones)

### Day 30 (Refresh)
- [ ] Create 2–3 new pin designs for top-performing Pinterest content
- [ ] Repost the Reel to Stories (Stories have a separate reach pool)
- [ ] Check which group posts got the most engagement — double down on that angle

## What NOT to Do

**Facebook:**
- Do NOT post affiliate links directly to Facebook — always link to a Verto page first
- Do NOT use link shorteners (bit.ly etc.) — FB demotes them
- Do NOT post identical content to multiple groups on the same day — you'll be flagged as spam
- Do NOT buy Facebook Page likes — these are worthless and hurt your reach rate
- Do NOT use engagement bait phrases ("like this if you agree") — against policy and demoted

**Pinterest:**
- Do NOT pin all your content to the same board at once — spread over days
- Do NOT use keyword stuffing in descriptions — Pinterest reads it as spam
- Do NOT create boards with 0–5 pins then abandon them — hurts account authority
- Do NOT use stock photos that Pinterest's AI has already indexed 1,000 times — original graphics only
- Do NOT skip UTM parameters — you will have no idea what's converting

## The One Metric That Matters Per Platform

**Facebook:** Cost Per Click to Verto (from organic posts)
If your posts aren't driving people to click through to the calculator, the copy needs work — not the format.

**Pinterest:** Monthly outbound clicks (tracked in Pinterest Analytics)
Not impressions, not saves — outbound clicks. That's the number that turns into revenue.
